Eco-Friendly Electronics

Did you know? In NYC you're required to recycle electronic waste items such as computers, phones, tablets, etc.

Electronic waste recycling, electronics recycling, or e-waste recycling is the disassembly and separation of components and raw materials of waste electronics; when referring to specific types of e-waste, the terms like computer recycling or mobile phone recycling may be used.

A guide outlining recommended practices for managing electronic devices.

Recycling electronics helps reduce pollution that would be generated while manufacturing a new product and the need to extract valuable and limited virgin resources. Electronic recycling also reduces the energy used in new product manufacturing.

  • Check for Recycling Programs: Look for electronic recycling programs in your area. Many communities offer drop-off locations or special collection events for electronics.
  • Prepare Devices for Recycling: Before recycling, remove any personal data from your devices. Wipe your devices clean and consider resetting them to factory settings.
  • Follow Local Regulations: Be aware of any local regulations or guidelines for electronic recycling. Some items, such as batteries or certain types of electronics, may have specific disposal requirements.
  • Spread Awareness: Encourage friends and family to recycle their electronic devices as well. By spreading awareness, you can help minimize electronic waste and protect the environment.

    Why Recycle Electronics? Electronic devices contain valuable materials such as metals, plastics, and glass. When these devices are not properly recycled, they end up in landfills, posing environmental risks such as soil and water contamination. Additionally, electronic waste often contains hazardous substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can leach into the environment if not disposed of correctly. By recycling your electronics with us, you're not only preventing harmful substances from polluting the environment but also ensuring that valuable materials are recovered and reused in the manufacturing of new products.
